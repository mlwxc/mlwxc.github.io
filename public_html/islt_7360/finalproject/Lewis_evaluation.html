Evaluation of Michael's Book Exploration

Evaluators

First Evaluator: Haley
She is a friend that knows a bit about design coding. Haley works with art quite a bit and I contacted her in order to get her opinion about the design aspects of the website. She also used her phone to test the usefulness of the website on a mobile system. She used an iPhone 7 with safari. The operating system it used was iOS 11.


Second Evaluator: Holden
Holden is a person who spends quite a bit of time on the internet web surfing. He is someone I go to when I need to find out how to do something technologically related that is out of my depth. He is knowledgeable in coding, web functions, and ways to make things easier for people to access. There isn’t anything computer related he doesn’t know how to do, at least a little bit. He uses a Windows 10 OS and his browser is the latest version of Google Chrome.


Third Evaluator: Justin
Justin is a friend of Holden’s. I wanted somebody I didn’t know in any way to look at the website and see if they liked the design of it. He told me he has no ability to code. I wanted someone that would signify an average end-user. Somebody that has no knowledge of the person that created the website and held no bias towards the person. He told me he was using a Windows 10 OS and the latest version of Mozilla Firefox.

Use Cases

My website has not met my use case scenarios. I don't have enough information for the use cases. I wanted to include author information, but that is not in there yet. Neither is there any book information yet. The purpose of the site was to create a place for everyone to go and find a book they wanted to read. But I don't have any information available for them yet. I don't have links to resources for them to look at books or authors. Neither do I have every genre available for exploration. I might have to abandon some items I wanted to get to, because the website would be impossible for one person to build by themselves, in the time I have.

Recommended Changes

I need to get the table of contents set up for ease of navigation. I also need to get graphics set into the webpage correctly. I might want to think about limiting myself to maybe one genre in fiction or nonfiction. This would give users a clear idea of what I would like to make. Focus on that genre and create a list of books in that area with authors for the books. I am hoping this will improve the usability of the website. It would also make the website more approachable and less daunting for the end-user.

Reflections on Evaluations

Doing these evaluations were necessary for me. I would have never realized how much was wrong with my website without people to point out the problems. I knew I needed to add a table of contents, but the background color was not something I would have thought would detract from the site. They showed me I need to be focused on more than just the content of the website, but the design as well. I, also, noticed how hard it is for people to find users to evaluate their websites. I spent most of my week attempting to find people to evaluate my work. This might be some of the reasons certain projects never get off of the ground.

Every person was incredibly helpful in pointing out the flaws. I needed more than one person's perspective. This is the problem I had in one of my previous assignments. I wasn't able to find multiple people to evaluate. I was able to find three people to give me some helpful use cases, but nothing more than that. I was really excited to find someone I didn't know to evaulate my site. They told me everything that was wrong with no reason to spare my feelings. Justin was the most useful person to help me with this assignment.
